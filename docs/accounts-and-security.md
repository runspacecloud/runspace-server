# Accounts and security

## Protect your account

Use a unique password that you do not use for another service.

Enable two-factor authentication when it is available for your account.

Never share:

- Your password
- Your account key
- Two-factor authentication codes
- Recovery codes
- Sign-in links intended only for you

RunSpace support should never ask you to send your password or complete
account key in a message.

## Account keys

An account key may be used as an important part of signing in or recovering
access.

Store it in a password manager or another protected location. Losing it may
make account recovery more difficult.

## Devices and sessions

The security settings may show devices or sessions connected to your
account.

Remove sessions you do not recognize and change your credentials if you
believe someone else gained access.

## Two-factor authentication

Two-factor authentication adds an additional verification step during
sign-in.

Save any recovery information before leaving the setup screen.

## Suspicious activity

Contact support when you notice:

- Sign-ins you do not recognize
- Unexpected password or key changes
- Messages sent without your knowledge
- Unknown devices connected to your account
