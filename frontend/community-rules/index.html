Community Rules

Rules that keep
RunSpace safe
for everyone.

RunSpace is built for private communication, trust, and control. These rules help keep the platform safe, respectful, and usable.

The goal is simple: protect privacy, reduce abuse, and keep RunSpace a place people can actually trust.

Respect people

No harassment, threats, stalking, bullying, or targeted abuse.

Protect privacy

Do not expose private information, messages, credentials, or sensitive personal data.

No platform abuse

No spam, scraping, automated abuse, rate-limit evasion, or trust-system manipulation.

Fair enforcement

Rules are enforced to protect users, not to control normal disagreement.

Rules

Respect others. Do not harass, threaten, stalk, bully, or target people. Disagreements are allowed. Abuse is not.

No illegal activity. Do not use RunSpace to plan, promote, distribute, or coordinate illegal activity, fraud, exploitation, or real-world harm.

No spam or platform abuse. Do not mass-message, scrape, automate abuse, evade rate limits, manipulate trust systems, or disrupt the service.

Protect privacy. Do not share someone else’s private information without permission, including private messages, addresses, credentials, or sensitive personal data.

Security research. Responsible security testing is welcome when it avoids harm, data access, persistence, spam, or service disruption.

Enforcement. RunSpace may limit, suspend, or remove accounts that break these rules. Serious abuse may result in permanent removal.

Appeals. If you believe an enforcement action was wrong, contact support with your username, what happened, and why you believe it should be reviewed.

What this means in practice
Private does not mean lawless. Privacy protects users. It does not protect abuse.
Reports are reviewed carefully. Context matters, but serious harm is handled quickly.
Rules may evolve. As RunSpace grows, these rules may be updated to protect the community.
